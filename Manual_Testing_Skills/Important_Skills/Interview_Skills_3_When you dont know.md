
Amazon is not testing memory.  
They are testing:

> **Can you think clearly under uncertainty?**

---

# 🧠 What Interviewers Actually Expect

When you don’t know an answer, they are NOT expecting:  
❌ perfect story  
❌ instant response

They are expecting:  
✅ calm thinking  
✅ structured response  
✅ honesty

---

# 🔥 What to Do When You Don’t Know the Answer

## ✅ Step 1 — Pause (This is powerful)

Say:

> “Let me think about a relevant example…”

👉 Take 5–10 seconds. This is NORMAL.

---

## ✅ Step 2 — Map to Closest Story

Even if question is new, map it to:

- SKU (ambiguity)
    
- E2E (complexity)
    
- Playwright (learning)
    

👉 80% of questions can be answered using these.

---

## ✅ Step 3 — Use This Universal Structure

If stuck, just follow this:

> “I may not have exact same situation, but a similar experience was…”

Then:

- Problem
    
- What you did
    
- Result
    

---

# 🧩 Example

## Question:

> “Tell me about a failure”

You didn’t prepare?

Say:

> “One situation that comes close is when I initially underestimated the complexity of Salesforce UI while trying to automate it…”

👉 Then continue.

---

# 🧠 If You REALLY Can’t Think of Anything

Use this:

> “I don’t recall an exact situation right now, but generally how I approach this is…”

👉 Then explain your thinking.

---

# ⚠️ What NOT to Do

### ❌ Panic + speak randomly

### ❌ Stay silent too long

### ❌ Say “I don’t know” and stop

---

# 🎯 Safe Recovery Sentence (Memorize This)

> “I may not have the exact scenario, but a similar situation I handled was…”

👉 This line alone can save your interview.

---

# 🧠 Real Insight (Very Important)

Strong candidates are not:

- the ones who know all answers
    

They are:

- the ones who **handle unknown questions calmly**
    

---

# 🔥 Final Mental Model

Think like this:

> “Whatever they ask → I will connect it to something I have done”
